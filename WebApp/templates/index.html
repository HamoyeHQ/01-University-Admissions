About the Project

The constant pursuit of a foreign education causes graduates all around the globe to apply for a masterâ€™s program in foreign universities. This process is not only challenging but also uncertain as most prospective students do not understand the conditions surrounding their chances of getting admitted into their choice schools.

From the EDA carried out on the dataset, a heatmap reveals that the feature parameters having the highest correlation to Chance of Admit (the target variable) are: CGPA, GRE Score, TOEFL Score, Research and University Rating.

Of these 5 variables, we know that the student can influence the CGPA, GRE, TOEFL and Research scores. However, the University Rating poses a different problem as it is difficult to explicitly ascertain the rating of a school for each academic year, as information as to how the ranking was obtained was not documented within the dataset. For this ambiguity, we have decided to train our model on the 4 key features that every student can actually influence, and with that try to predict the chance of admittance of the student.

Having conclusively developed a model that best suits the dataset and task at hand, we designed a web-based interface to enable end-users to interact with the model without actually worrying about all the specifics.
